ðŸ”¢ Understanding Fertilizer Numbers

Fertilizer bags show three numbers like 21-0-0 or 10-10-10:

  • First number = Nitrogen (N) percentage
  • Second number = Phosphorus (P) percentage
  • Third number = Potassium (K) percentage

Example: 21-0-0 means 21% Nitrogen. If applying nitrogen, enter 21 in the Fertilizer % field.

ðŸŒ¾ Common Organic vs Synthetic Options

For Nitrogen:

  • Organic: Blood meal (13%), Feather meal (~10%), Fish emulsion (5%)
  • Synthetic: Ammonium sulfate (21%), Urea (~46%)

For Phosphorus:

  • Organic: Bone meal (15%), Rock phosphate (~3%)
  • Synthetic: Triple superphosphate (~45%)

For Potassium:

  • Organic: Lanbeinite - Sulfate of Potash (~22%)
  • Synthetic: Muriate of Potash (~60%)

For Sulfur:

  • Organic: Sulfur Fungicide - Elemental Sulfur (~90%)
  • (note:Microbes in the soil digest the sulfur and produce sulfuric acid which in turn lowers the pH. It is a relatively slow process.)

For Boron:

  • Organic: Borax - Sodium Borate (~11.4%)
  • (note: Using this will leave the sodium behind in the soil.)
